What Should I Expect From a Lead Generation Service?

Setting Realistic Expectations Before You Hire

← Back to Blog

If you are a contractor in NW Pennsylvania, a roofer, an HVAC tech, a landscaper, or a general contractor, and you are thinking about hiring a lead generation service, the first thing I want to tell you is this: I get it. You are busy. You are good at your trade. And you just want the phone to ring with real jobs from real people who can actually pay.

That is a fair thing to want. But before you hand over a dollar to anyone, including me, you deserve a straight answer to the question most lead gen companies dance around: what should you actually expect from local lead generation for contractors?

The Guarantee Myth

The most common assumption I hear from contractors exploring lead gen is that they are buying a guarantee. A guarantee on calls. A guarantee on jobs. A guarantee on revenue. I understand why, because that is what the pitch usually sounds like.

Here is the honest truth: no legitimate lead generation service can guarantee results the way you are imagining. What they can do, and what I do, is build the infrastructure, handle the local SEO, drive the right traffic, and put real local prospects in front of you. What happens next depends on you.

Lead generation is not a vending machine. You do not put money in and get jobs out. It is a system, and like any system, it only works when both sides show up.

Your Responsibility in This Partnership

This is the part most lead gen companies leave out of the conversation, and it is the part I think matters most. Before a lead generation service can work for you, you need two things in place on your end.

The first is a genuine desire to grow, not just talk about it. Growth means accepting new work, adjusting your schedule, and sometimes being uncomfortable for a season.

The second is the staff and resources to handle volume. A lead that goes unanswered is a lead that goes to your competitor. If you cannot answer the phone, respond to a form submission, or follow up within a few hours, even the best roofing leads in NW Pennsylvania will not convert. It does not matter how good the source is.

I would rather have that conversation upfront and lose the sale than watch a good contractor fail because they were not ready for what they asked for.

A Realistic Timeline: What to Expect Month by Month

One of the biggest questions contractors ask is how to get more leads and, more importantly, how fast. Here is how I think about the growth curve for a local service business working with a lead gen partner.

Months One and Two

This is the foundation phase. Sites go live, directories get populated, and Google starts paying attention. Do not expect a flood of calls yet. Expect the groundwork to get solid. This is where the long term work gets done quietly in the background.

Months Three and Four

Leads start trickling in. You are testing your follow up process, learning what questions prospects ask, and tightening your close rate. This is where your responsiveness matters most. The contractors who win at this stage are the ones who pick up the phone.

Months Five and Six

This is where the investment starts to pay off. A realistic target, one I work toward with every client, is a 10x return on your monthly investment by this stage. That is not a promise. It is a benchmark we build toward together with consistent effort on both sides.

What to Look for in a Lead Generation Partner

Not every lead gen company operates the same way, and some red flags are worth knowing before you sign anything.

Watch out for companies that:

  • Jump straight to pricing before asking about your business, your capacity, or your goals
  • Make guarantees that sound too good to be true, because they are
  • Lock you into long term contracts before proving the relationship works
  • Go quiet after onboarding and leave you guessing about what is happening

A good lead gen partner asks hard questions before they take your money. They want to know if you are actually a good fit for the service, because if you are not, it does not work for either of you.

The Red Flag That Matters Most

If a lead generation company is only worried about their bottom line and not whether the service is helping your business, that is the clearest sign to walk away. I have seen it happen. A vendor sells the dream, collects the monthly fee, and moves on to the next contractor whether the first one is succeeding or not.

That is not a partnership. That is a transaction with a subscription attached to it.

The companies worth working with are the ones who are honest about fit before the contract is signed. They would rather send you somewhere else than take your money and underdeliver.

Lead Generation Is a Two Way Street

The relationship between a contractor and a lead gen service has to be built on honesty and communication. If something feels off, say something. Address it directly. Give it a fair chance to be corrected.

And if it cannot be corrected, move on. Find someone else. That is not failure. That is good business sense.

Which brings me to something I feel strongly about: no long term contracts. I do not believe in locking a small business owner into a 12 month commitment before either of us has proven the relationship works. If what I am doing is working, you will want to stay. If it is not, you should be free to go. It is that simple.

The best lead gen partnerships work because both sides are honest, both sides show up, and neither side is afraid to say when something is not working.
